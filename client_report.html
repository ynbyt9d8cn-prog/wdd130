Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use? I used the site plan to choose content that clearly explained the purpose of the site and supported the theme. I was very slow to select the images and send them to my lead dev so in the end it was my fault that they didn't make it into the website.

How did you communicate with your developer? Any issues? I communicated by sharing the site plan, links, and a little feedback as pages were published. Communication was mostly smooth, and when issues came up, they were usually related to waiting for files to be pushed before work could continue. Overall the developers did a great job at keeping things clear with me

Did your developer meet your expectations? Yes. The homepage was published correctly and updated with CSS styling to match the site plan and wireframe. I did a poor job at comunicating my vision so I can't expect anything better than what was done.

What will you do differently next time? Next time I would set clearer deadlines and check-in times, and I would confirm file structure (CSS file name, image folder, link paths) earlier so fewer small issues slow down progress. Also now that I have done it I feel so much more confident to work with github and share what I am doing with others. I for sure would do a much better job.

General Experience and Reflections

Most challenging part: The most challenging part was coordinating timing and making sure everyone had pushed the files needed before the next person could work. I take responsibilty for my errors and late start, this caused me to be behind and have to play catch up which was a problem. Also learning how to use github correctly took me quite some time. Now I feel much better with it though.

What did you enjoy most? I enjoyed seeing the site go from a plan to a real published project. It also felt like a real world project which I loved. Having to work with people and deadlines was frustrating and hard but I feel like I learned the most from that. I also love working on multible sites at once to see how well they are working and diffrent levels of coding play out to make them.

What would you do differently? I would create a shared checklist at the start and confirm consistent naming (CSS files, folders, and links) so the team stays aligned. I would also have asked for more help. I got kinda scared and this caused me to get behind and avoid the class. If I would have just locked in and done my work I feel like it would have ended much better in the end.

Do you feel more confident now? Yes. This project made me more confident working with GitHub, communicating tasks, and contributing to a team project with real workflow steps like pull, commit, push, and publish. I loved it and am greatfull for it even though it was very hard for me to work with others in a job I am not confident in. Thank you for the great learing experinces.
